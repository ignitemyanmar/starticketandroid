1 . Visa

For details on visa requirement to enter The Republic of Union of Myanmar, please visit the website of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs at www.mofa.gov.mm or contact your nearest The Embassy of the Republic of The Union of Myanmar directly. Visa application is available upon arrival at Naypyitaw International Airport in Naypyitaw , Yangon International Airport in Yangon and Mandalay International Airport in Mandalay. The visa is only valid for fifteen days.The cost is Approx US$ 20 (method of payment is cash). You are also required to provide at least one photograph.

Countries Approved for Visa on Arrival

The visitors are required to enter from Yangon International Airport.

Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Viet Nam, Australia, China, Denmark, France, Germany, India, Italy, Japan, Republic of Korea, New Zealand, Norway, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, United Kingdom, United States of America. Additionally, the citizens of the following 22 countries will be able to apply On-Arrival Visa starting from 1-2-2013: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Canada, Cyprus, Czech, Estonia, Finland, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Israel, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, the Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia and Slovenia.

Please check the www.mofa.gov.mm for updates of the countries list and detail of obtaining the visa.

2 . Internet and Telecommunications

There are free wifi zones in Yangon provided by RedLink During SEA Games: Thuwunna Stadium, Thuwunna Football field, Thein Phyu Stadium, North Dagon Shooting Range and MCC Hall.

Normally, visitors can go to internet caf√© for using internet and the rates vary from 300-500 kyats per hour.

To call overseas, one can use Skype or G-talk in internet shops or Y-Talk cards and other pay phones distributed around Yangon by MPT.

3 . Cash

Visitors can use the credit cards here. US Dollars, Singapore Dollars and Euros are the easiest to exchange here but the notes that are clean without folding are most preferred by the money changes including banks. You can see your Money Changer list in shopping category.

4 . Transportation

In Yangon, public transportation is via buses, city train, taxis and boats (to Dala region). Cycling is permitted but motor cycles are not allowed.

In Mandalay, public transportation is via motor cycles and very few car taxis but there are a lot of motor cycle taxis.

5 . Hotel Bookings

In tourist seasons, you need to book the hotels in advanced as there are very few hotels and rooms are normally not enough. The hotel rates can go double in some tourist seasons.
